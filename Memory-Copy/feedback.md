**Be precise about what papers say.** Nate catches misreadings (e.g., per-row vs shared ŷ, spectral vs L∞ for boundary, Kim vs Tyler parameter counting). Always distinguish "the paper says X" from "I infer X."

**Why:** v1.1 had several claims that misread the source papers; Nate corrected all of them carefully. Accuracy matters more than confident-sounding assertions.
**How to apply:** When summarizing a paper, quote directly or caveat clearly. If uncertain, say so.

---

**Do not assert a "deviation" from prior work without evidence.** Nate pushes back on framing his group's choices (per-row ŷ, discarding n/δ, spectral boundary optimization) as "mistakes" or "deviations" unless there is a clear documented reason.

**Why:** These were deliberate choices with reasoning, not accidents.
**How to apply:** Frame differences from published methods as "our approach differs in X; here is why that is principled."

---

**When corrections are needed, provide a revised derivation, not just a flag.** Nate wants v1.2-style re-derivations (e.g., re-do free-parameter count from scratch), not just acknowledgments that v1.1 was wrong.

**Why:** The goal is a planning document that is correct and actionable, not a list of acknowledged errors.
**How to apply:** When correcting a prior claim, provide the corrected analysis inline.

---

**Provide step-by-step next steps for each open question.** Nate explicitly wants each track to have concrete steps: what to code, what to read, what to test, what to interpret.

**Why:** E2 response in review_followup.md: "I would like v1.2... to provide a clear outline of which avenues/questions are left to address and pursue. For each of these, I would like a step-by-step of what to do next."
**How to apply:** Always end each track/topic section with ordered, concrete actions.
